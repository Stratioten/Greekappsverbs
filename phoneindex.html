How to 'Ace'ient Greek: Verbs

Here you can revise a whole bunch of tenses, active, middle and passive in -ω, -εω, -αω and irregular verbs including:

  1. Present tense
  2. Future tense

  4. 1st Aorist tense

Please note(for future tense):

If a verb stem ends in 'κ' adding a '-σ' to the stem makes a 'ξ'. Same with 'γ' and '-σ'.

If a verb stem ends in 'π' adding a '-σ' to the stem makes a 'ψ'. Same with 'φ' and '-σ'.

Also because 'σ-κ-σ' and 'θ-σ' are hard to pronounce the first sigma and the theta drop out so: διδασκ goes to διδαξ-ω and πειθ goes to πει-σ-ω

Top 10 irregular aorists:

  • εἷλον, I took, (αἱρέω)
  • ἀπέθανον, I died; I was killed, (ἀποθνῄσκω)
  • ἐγενόμην, I became, (γίγνομαι)
  • ἦλθον, I came; I went, (ἔρχομαι)
  • ηὗρον, I found, (εὑρίσκω)
  • ἔσχον, I had, (ἔχω)
  • ἔλαβον, I took; I captured, (λαμβάνω)
  • εἶπον, I said, (λέγω)
  • εἶδον, I saw, (ὁράω)
  • ἥνεγκα, I carried, (φέρω)

Verbs (2nd Aorist-εω)

A few verbs in -εω do have 2nd (strong) aorists (ἀφικνομην, ἀφικνεομαι; εἱλον, αἱρεω) but when declining these verbs do not involve contraction, use 2nd Aorist (-ω) to revise these

Irregular aorists -εω:
  • εἷλον, I took, (αἱρέω)
  • ἀφικομην,I arrived, (ἀφικνεομαι)
  • ἐκαλεσα, I called; I invited, (καλεω)
  • ἐπλευσα, I sail, (πλεω)
  • ὑποσχον, I promise, (ὑπισχνεομαι (+fut inf))

Verbs (2nd Aorist-αω)

A few verbs in -αω do have 2nd (strong) aorists (but only one in Greek to Gcse: εἰδον, ὁραω) but when declining these verbs do not involve contraction, use 2nd Aorist (-ω) to revise these

Verbs with some kind of irregularity -αω:
  • Strong aorist: εἰδον, I saw, (ὁραω)
  • Forms that take alpha instead of eta before the sigma in the future and aorist
    • ἐγελασα (γελαω)
    • ἐπειραθην (πειραομαι)
  • Change in the stem: ἐχρησαμην, I use (+ dat), (χραομαι)
